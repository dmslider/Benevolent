House System

The House system is a cornerstone of school life at Benevolent Islamic Secondary School, creating smaller communities within our larger school family. Each student belongs to one of four houses, which compete in academics, sports, arts, and service throughout the year. The house system fosters school spirit, encourages friendly competition, and builds lasting friendships across year groups.

House Organisation

Each house is led by a House Master/Mistress and supported by House Prefects and senior leaders. Students are assigned to houses either by selection process or during their first year, creating vertical communities where students from Forms 1-4 study and compete together. This structure allows older students to mentor younger ones and builds a strong sense of unity.

Our Four Houses

Each house has its own identity, colours, and traditions:

  • Alfard House: Known for academic excellence and scholarly achievement
  • Jahangir House: Recognized for sporting prowess and athletic excellence
  • Makiya House: Celebrated for creativity, arts, and cultural contributions
  • Nizam House: Distinguished for service, leadership, and community engagement

Inter-House Competitions

Throughout the school year, houses compete across multiple domains:

  • Academic Competitions: Quiz competitions, debate tournaments, and academic excellence awards
  • Sports Fixtures: Football matches, athletics meet, basketball competitions, and swimming events
  • Cultural Events: Drama competitions, music performances, and talent showcases
  • House Assemblies: Special gatherings and house-specific programmes
  • Community Service: Social responsibility projects and charitable activities

Benefits of the House System

Membership in a house provides numerous benefits to students:

  • Sense of Belonging: Creating a family-like atmosphere within the larger school
  • Cross-Year Relationships: Building friendships and mentoring relationships across age groups
  • Leadership Development: Opportunities to take on house leadership roles and responsibilities
  • School Spirit: Fostering pride in one's house and school community
  • Healthy Competition: Encouraging students to do their best in a supportive, friendly competitive environment
  • Personal Development: Supporting individual growth through team participation

House Points and Awards

Houses earn points throughout the year for:

  • Academic achievement and exam results
  • Sporting victories and athletic performance
  • Participation in cultural and artistic events
  • Community service and charitable work
  • Exemplary behaviour and discipline
  • Attendance and punctuality

House Champions

At the end of each school year, the house with the most points is declared Overall House Champions and celebrated during the school's Prize Giving ceremony. House Champions are recognized with trophies, special privileges, and lasting recognition in school history.

House Spirit and Traditions

Each house develops its own unique culture and traditions, including:

  • House colours, mottos, and symbols
  • House songs and chants
  • Special house gatherings and socials
  • House mascots and traditions
